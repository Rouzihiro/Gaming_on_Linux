# First Aid Leveling 1-300 and Recipes Guide - WoW Classic
*By Wowhead | Updated: 2025/01/25*

## üìú Overview
**First Aid** is a **secondary profession** in WoW Classic (alongside **Fishing** and **Cooking**). It allows players to craft and use **bandages** to heal themselves and others, and **Anti-Venom** to remove poison effects.

*   **Maximum Skill:** 300
*   **Why Learn It:** Reduces downtime while leveling, conserves healer mana in groups, and provides crucial self-heals in PvP or for classes without healing spells.
*   **Key Materials:** **Cloth** (drops from humanoids) and **Venom Sacs** (drops from poisonous creatures).

This guide covers trainer locations, recipe acquisition, and a clear path to level First Aid from 1 to 300.

## üìà Leveling Path (1-300)

### Skill Colors & Chance to Gain a Skill Point
*   **Orange:** 100% chance.
*   **Yellow:** High chance.
*   **Green:** Low chance.
*   **Grey:** No chance.

| Material | Quantity |

| **Silk Cloth** | 160 |
| **Mageweave Cloth** | 120 |
| **Runecloth** | 100 |

*   **Vendor Purchases Required:**
    *   **Expert First Aid - Under Wraps**
    *   **Manual: Heavy Silk Bandage**
    *   **Manual: Mageweave Bandage**

### Phase 1: Apprentice to Journeyman (1 - 125)
**Required Cloth:** 160-180 Linen Cloth, 100-120 Wool Cloth.

1.  **Train Apprentice First Aid.**
2.  Craft **Linen Bandage** from skill **1 to 40**.
3.  At skill 40, train **Heavy Linen Bandage**.
4.  Craft **Heavy Linen Bandage** from skill **41 to 50**.
5.  **Train Journeyman First Aid.**
6.  Craft **Heavy Linen Bandage** from skill **51 to 80**.
7.  At skill 80, train **Wool Bandage** and **Anti-Venom**.
8.  Craft **Wool Bandage** from skill **81 to 115**.
9.  At skill 115, train **Heavy Wool Bandage**.
10. Craft **Heavy Wool Bandage** from skill **116 to 125**.

### Phase 2: Expert (125 - 225)
**Required Cloth:** 100-120 Wool Cloth, 140-160 Silk Cloth, 20 Mageweave Cloth.

1.  Purchase and use **Expert First Aid - Under Wraps** to learn **Expert First Aid**.
2.  Craft **Heavy Wool Bandage** from skill **126 to 150**.
3.  At skill 150, train **Silk Bandage**.
4.  Craft **Silk Bandage** from skill **151 to 180**.
5.  Use **Manual: Heavy Silk Bandage** to learn the recipe.
6.  Craft **Heavy Silk Bandage** from skill **181 to 210**.
7.  Use **Manual: Mageweave Bandage** to learn the recipe.
8.  Craft **Mageweave Bandage** from skill **211 to 225**.

### Phase 3: Artisan (225 - 300) *Requires Level 35*
**Required Cloth:** 80-100 Mageweave Cloth, 80-100 Runecloth.

**Prerequisite:** Complete the **"Triage"** quest at level 35 with First Aid 225 to learn **Artisan First Aid**.

1.  Craft **Mageweave Bandage** from skill **226 to 240**.
2.  Train **Heavy Mageweave Bandage** from the quest doctor (Dr. VanHowzen or Dr. Victor).
3.  Craft **Heavy Mageweave Bandage** from skill **241 to 260**.
4.  Train **Runecloth Bandage** from the doctor.
5.  Craft **Runecloth Bandage** from skill **261 to 290**.
6.  Train **Heavy Runecloth Bandage** from the doctor.
7.  Craft **Heavy Runecloth Bandage** from skill **290 to 300**.

**Note:** You can grind **Heavy Mageweave Bandage** all the way to 300 if you have abundant Mageweave Cloth.

### Artisan First Aid Quest: "Triage"
**Requirements:** Level 35 & First Aid Skill 225.

*   **Alliance:** Start with **"Alliance Trauma"** from Nissa Firestone in Ironforge, or go directly to **Doctor Gustaf VanHowzen** in Theramore, Dustwallow Marsh (67.6, 48.8).
*   **Horde:** Start with **"Horde Trauma"** from Arnok in Orgrimmar, or go directly to **Doctor Gregory Victor** in Hammerfall, Arathi Highlands (73.4, 36.8).

**Quest Objective:** Save 15 injured soldiers using provided **Triage Bandages** without letting 6 die. Prioritize **Critically Injured** soldiers first. Completing this quest teaches **Artisan First Aid**.

**Important Note:** After the quest, you must return to the doctor at skill levels **240**, **260**, and **290** to learn the next bandage recipes (**Heavy Mageweave Bandage**, **Runecloth Bandage**, **Heavy Runecloth Bandage**).
